The operating model — 4DX
One WIG · lead measures · the scoreboard · the cadence
Last week we put a name to something we'd all been feeling: the cost of running fast without a shared system. 4DX isn't a new methodology we're adopting — it's the structure that turns what we already agreed on into something we can actually measure and hold together.
The Wildly Important Goal: grow high-MQL leads that convert into trials. One north star. Owned collectively. Everything else serves it.
Lead measures: two to three predictive, controllable actions per team — built into Airtable and tracked weekly. Not lag measures that tell us what already happened. Lead measures that tell us whether we're doing the things that will move the number. The scoreboard: this document, Airtable, and the ELT brief. Everyone sees the same data, always. No version control. No guessing what the latest number is.
The cadence: Wednesday office hours. Fifteen minutes. Not a meeting — a rhythm. A commitment. The six-step quarter: team process audit, WIG working session, define lead measures, build the scoreboard, launch the cadence, first retrospective at 30 days. Then iterate every month and keep going.
Slow down to speed up. The operating model that makes everything else stick.
[2:45]
The weekly rhythm
Monday through Friday · what happens when · why it works
The weekly operating rhythm is what makes the whole system run.
Monday: pull, validate, and review. ELT data pull, validate all sources, flag anomalies, week-over-week movement scan, top-12-countries quick review, dual reporting due. The week starts with clean data or it doesn't start at all.
Tuesday: what moved and why. Deep dive on what went up and what went down. Pull Airtable campaign context. Cross-reference with initiative activity. Draft the preliminary narrative — not just the numbers but the story behind them. Wednesday: build the story and team review. Organize and deepen the narrative. Tie data to business context and OKRs. Office hours on all projects. Build the presentation structure. This is the day the data becomes a decision.
Thursday: polish and present. Final tweaks. Rachel presents to the ELT team. Afternoon: all-projects review and side tickets. Friday: look ahead. Review what the week surfaced. Plan next week's focus. Update Airtable initiative progress. Prep the Monday pull infrastructure.
Monday to Friday. Every week. The rhythm is the system.

I want to close with something personal. I've spent my career learning how to make complicated things make sense. I bring both of those things here. My goal isn't to make things feel complicated — it's to do the complicated work so that when we're in the room together, everything feels simple and obvious and possible. That's the job. That's what I'm here for.
